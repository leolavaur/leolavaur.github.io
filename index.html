Hi, I’m Léo

I am a researcher at the SnT (Interdisciplinary Centre for Security, Reliability and Trust). I received my Ph.D. in 2024 from IMT Atlantique. Before that, I received an engineering degree in information security from ENSIBS and worked at Orange Cyberdefense as a part-time employee for three years.

Research

My research interests span different aspects of distributed systems, with a focus on network and system security, as well as decentralized and federated learning. During my Ph.D., I applied federated learning to the collaborative training of intrusion detection models, studying its limits in terms of data heterogeneity and resistance to Byzantine contributions. We proposed counters measures to ensure the quality of the global models, as well as a new way to generate datasets for distributed intrusion detection. I am now focusing on relationship extraction and inference under uncertainty in distributed systems, dealing with challenges of partial observability. I am also interested on the network optimization of decentralized learning in the context of constrained environments. Alongside my research, I taught topics related to network security to Master’s and Engineering students, worked on scientific popularization, and now contribute to the organization of national and international workshops and conferences.
